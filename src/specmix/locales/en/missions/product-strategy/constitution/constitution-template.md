## 2. Core Values

### 2.1 Customer First

- Customer value is the starting point for all decisions
- Prioritize customer voice over assumptions
- Focus on customer's Job-to-be-Done

### 2.2 Data-Driven

- Make decisions based on data, not intuition
- Culture of forming and validating hypotheses
- Measure success with metrics

### 2.3 Fast Learning

- Market validation over perfection
- Learn from failures
- Continuous experimentation and improvement

### 2.4 Clear Communication

- Concise and clear documentation
- Transparent decision-making process
- Stakeholder alignment

---

## 3. Product Principles

### 3.1 Problem Definition First

- Deeply understand the problem before solutions
- Ask "why" five times
- Solve root causes, not symptoms

### 3.2 Pursue Simplicity

- Focus on core value over adding features
- Complexity is the enemy - keep it simple
- Focus on 20% of features used by 80% of users

### 3.3 Measurable Success

- Define success metrics before launch
- Connect every feature to KPIs
- Opinions without data are just opinions

### 3.4 User Experience First

- UX quality over technical constraints
- Consistent user experience
- Consider accessibility and inclusivity

## 4. Decision-Making Framework

### 4.1 Prioritization Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Customer Impact | 40% | How much value for how many customers |
| Strategic Alignment | 30% | How well it aligns with vision and goals |
| Feasibility | 20% | Is it realistically executable |
| Cost Efficiency | 10% | Is ROI appropriate |

### 4.2 Trade-off Decision Guide

| Situation | Prefer | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Features vs Quality | Quality | Low quality damages trust |
| Speed vs Completeness | Learning Speed | Fast validation is more important |
| Growth vs Revenue | [Context-dependent] | Decide based on stage |
| New vs Existing Customers | [Context-dependent] | Decide based on current goals |

### 4.3 What We Don't Do

- Adding features without customer value
- Large investments without validation
- Sacrificing long-term value for short-term revenue
- Copying competitors

## 5. Documentation Standards

### 5.1 6-Pager Writing Principles

- **Be Specific**: Numbers and examples instead of abstract statements
- **Be Data-Driven**: Cite sources, distinguish assumptions from facts
- **Be Concise**: Exclude unnecessary content
- **Be Actionable**: Next actions must be clear

### 5.2 Required Elements

All product documents must include:

- [ ] Customer problem definition
- [ ] Success metrics (KPIs)
- [ ] Target customer segment
- [ ] Competitive advantage
- [ ] Risks and mitigation plans

### 5.3 Review Criteria

Checklist before document approval:

- [ ] Are data sources cited?
- [ ] Are assumptions clearly marked?
- [ ] Are risks sufficiently considered?
- [ ] Are next steps clear?

## 6. Review & Approval Process

### 6.1 Review Stages

| Stage | Reviewer | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Self Review | Author | Completeness, consistency |
| Peer Review | Fellow PM | Logic, data |
| Leader Review | PM Leader | Strategic alignment |
| Stakeholder Review | Related teams | Feasibility |
| Final Approval | Decision maker | Go/No-go |

### 6.2 Feedback Guide

Characteristics of good feedback:

- Suggest specific improvements
- Provide opinions with evidence
- Offer alternatives
- Maintain constructive tone
